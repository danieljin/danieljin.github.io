Content Cycles

How is a Content Cycle Structured?

Content cycles are 12-week series of professional learning sessions organized around a single topic. These content cycles will be key resources for content leaders as they plan the collaborative time with their teams each week. Content cycles are customized to be curricular specific. Additional customizations will be supported at the school and team level by coaches.

A full content cycle guides teacher professional learning on a particular topic over approximately 12 weeks. Within the 12-week content cycle, teachers investigate four sub-topics by engaging in one week of shared learning, one week applying shared learning through lesson planning and lesson rehearsal, and one week analyzing data to monitor student progress.

What resources are included in a Content Cycle?

The sessions are built so that content leaders can focus more time and energy on internalizing and adapting sessions and reviewing teacher artifacts. Content leaders will receive comprehensive materials for leading content cycles, including weekly session plans with key messages and talking points, as well as powerpoints, exemplars, and handouts. While these resources are intended as a robust support, content leaders should feel free to make adjustments to session plans and materials based on the needs of their teams.
